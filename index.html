Crime Against Women in India

India is purported to be a country known for sexual violence against women, according to the Thomson Reuters Foundation 2018 survey[1]. India is called Bharat Mata (Hindi, from Sanskrit Bhāratāmbā) meaning Mother India in English, a country whose name itself Signifies the importance of women. Despite the symbolism, women in India face a lot of hardships. Around 130,000 - 220,000 cases of crime against women have been reported annually between the years 2001-2012 with a steadily increasing trend. 65% of Indian men believe women should tolerate violence in order to keep the family together, and women sometimes deserve to be beaten[2].Watch this poetic performance by Mariyam Saigal as she talks about the Crime against women.

This visualization tool aims to make people aware of the crimes committed against women in India. Some of the top crimes committed against women in India are- Domestic Violence, Sexual Harassment, Rape and Kidnapping. 

The following data is presented in this tool-

  1. The number of reported crime cases in each state.
  2. The crime rate (number of crime victims per 100,000 women), which is independent of the size of the population of a state.
  3. The number of cases being reported, the number of people getting arrested and the number of people actually being convicted. 
  4. States which are the most prominent for a particular crime.
